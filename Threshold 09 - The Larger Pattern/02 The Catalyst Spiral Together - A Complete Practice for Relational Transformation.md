# The Catalyst Spiral Together: *A Complete Practice for Relational Transformation*

---

Hello, dear companions.

Relationships grow through crisis.

Not through constant smooth sailing — though we all wish for that — but through the charged moments where something is at stake, where things could go either way, where what we do next will matter. These catalyst moments are where transformation actually happens. The question is: can we navigate them consciously?

The Catalyst Spiral maps the journey of relational transformation — from first sensing that something is happening, through the intensity of a charged moment, through reconnection and realignment, to lasting change. It's a journey we'll travel many times together. Each time, we return to familiar territory but at a new level — more skilled, more connected, more capable of meeting what arises.

~Matthew and Naomi Danielle Morningstar

## How we use this guide

We created this guide to be read and practiced together — by partners, friends, family members, or anyone who wants to develop their shared capacity to navigate the transformative moments that every relationship encounters.

We recommend:

- **Reading together**, aloud or in turn
- **Pausing often** to discuss what resonates or raises questions
- **Practicing as we go**, using the guide when we're not in crisis to build understanding
- **Returning regularly**, because navigating transformation is not something we master once but something we keep deepening

We've structured this guide based on the **Golden Triangle**, a framework that helps us understand and consciously shape our experiences. By focusing on three key aspects — **Experience**, **Process**, and **Practice** — we can gain a deeper understanding of our relational world and learn to cultivate the states we want to live in together.

- **Experience** is the felt sense itself: what it feels like to move through the stages of transformation, from first awareness through integration and celebration.
- **Process** is how that experience develops and unfolds: the eight stages, how they flow into each other, and how the spiral builds over time.
- **Practice** is what we actively do to cultivate skillful navigation: the concrete ways we navigate each stage and move through the complete cycle.

The Golden Triangle recognizes the dynamic, cyclical nature of these elements, with each one supporting and enhancing the others in an ongoing process of growth and transformation.

The "we" throughout this guide refers to all of us — whoever is reading and practicing together right now.

We suggest limiting yourself to 1-2 practices at a time, giving each one space to become a natural part of how we are with each other before moving on to the next.

## The catalyst spiral is an experience

*The catalyst spiral is what it feels like to move through relational transformation — from first sensing that something is happening, through the intensity of a charged moment, through reconnection, to lasting change.*

### The felt sense of each stage

**Awareness.** A subtle quality of noticing — sensing the relational field, perceiving what's present between us. There's a receptive openness, a listening quality.

**Dissonance.** Something is off. Friction. Tension. The sense that we're out of sync. It can be obvious or subtle, but it's there — a signal that something needs attention.

**Catalyst.** Energy concentrates. The stakes feel elevated. There's a sense of standing at a crossroads — the outcome isn't determined, and what we do matters.

**Pause.** Space opens. The urgent pressure to react eases. There's breathing room — a gap between what just happened and what happens next.

**Attunement.** Reaching toward each other. Seeking to understand. The felt sense of reconnection as we realign our inner worlds.

**Calibration.** Getting concrete. Making commitments. The grounded quality of translating understanding into specific action.

**Integration.** Practice. The sometimes awkward, sometimes natural process of making new patterns stick. There's patience here, and persistence.

**Celebration.** Acknowledgment. The warm sense of honoring what we've accomplished. Looking back on the journey with appreciation.

### The overall arc

The complete spiral has a recognizable arc:

It begins with **receptivity** — sensing what's present, noticing what's off. It moves through **intensity** — the catalyst moment where energy concentrates and stakes rise. It transitions through **reconnection** — pausing, attuning, realigning. It culminates in **embodiment** — calibrating, integrating, and finally celebrating.

When we've traveled the spiral consciously, there's a distinctive feeling of *completion*. Something has been worked through. The relationship has grown. We are different — in good ways — from who we were at the beginning.

### When we're conscious of the spiral

Moving through the spiral with awareness feels like:

- **Orientation.** We know where we are. We can name the stage.
- **Appropriate response.** We're doing what the current stage calls for, not forcing a different stage's work.
- **Trust in the process.** We know transformation has a shape. We trust we'll get through.
- **Participation.** We're actively navigating, not just being swept along.

### When we're unconscious of the spiral

Moving through without awareness feels like:

- **Disorientation.** We don't know what's happening or what to do.
- **Mismatched response.** We're trying to calibrate when we haven't attuned. We're trying to celebrate when integration hasn't happened.
- **Chaos.** It feels random, overwhelming, out of control.
- **Being dragged.** We're not navigating — we're being pulled through.

## The catalyst spiral is a process

*The spiral moves through eight recognizable stages, each creating conditions for the next, building capacity over many cycles.*

### The eight stages

**Stage 1: Developing Awareness.** *Sensing our relational field.* Before we can work with what's happening between us, we need to sense it. This stage develops our capacity to perceive the quality of our connection — its temperature, its openness, its flow. We're building baseline sensitivity, learning to notice before we name, developing shared language for what we sense. This stage produces the ability to perceive what's happening between us — the foundation for everything that follows.

**Stage 2: Recognizing Dissonance.** *Noticing when we're out of sync.* With awareness developed, we can now recognize when something is off — when we're misaligned, experiencing friction, out of sync. We're sensing dissonance early, before it becomes intense, identifying what kind of dissonance it is, naming it without blame. This stage produces clear recognition that our relational field needs attention.

**Stage 3: The Catalyst Moment.** *Meeting the crossroads where change becomes possible.* Not every dissonance becomes a catalyst. But sometimes enough energy accumulates — through conflict, revelation, vulnerability, truth — that we arrive at a threshold. Energy is heightened, stakes feel elevated, multiple outcomes seem possible. This stage produces the concentrated energy needed for transformation.

**Stage 4: Pause and Reflect.** *Creating space before reacting.* In the charged energy of a catalyst moment, our nervous systems want to react — fight, flee, freeze, appease. The pause interrupts this automatic sequence. It creates a gap between stimulus and response where conscious choice becomes possible. This stage produces the space where wisdom can arise.

**Stage 5: Attunement.** *Reconnecting and realigning our understanding.* From the paused place, we turn toward each other. Attunement is the process of sensing each other accurately and realigning our understanding. We're actively trying to understand each other's experience, checking our perceptions rather than assuming. This stage produces realignment — the felt sense of being back in sync.

**Stage 6: Calibration.** *Adjusting our actions to match our understanding.* Calibration is where understanding becomes concrete. We translate what we've learned through attunement into specific changes — behaviors we'll start or stop, patterns we'll adjust. This stage produces clear commitments and plans for change — the bridge between knowing and doing.

**Stage 7: Integration.** *Embodying new patterns until they become natural.* Integration is the sustained work of making new patterns stick. We practice new behaviors consistently until they become automatic — until they're no longer something we're trying to do but simply how we are together. This stage produces embodied change.

**Stage 8: Celebration and Reassessment.** *Honoring growth and charting direction.* We pause to acknowledge what we've accomplished. We take stock of where we are. We recognize the growth, appreciate the effort, and assess what remains. Then we set direction for the next cycle. This stage produces completion and preparation for what's next.

### How the stages flow

The stages have a natural sequence: Awareness → Dissonance → Catalyst → Pause → Attunement → Calibration → Integration → Celebration.

Each stage creates conditions for the next: we can't recognize dissonance without awareness; catalyst moments emerge from accumulated dissonance; pause creates space for attunement to occur; attunement reveals what needs calibrating; calibration sets up what needs to be integrated; integration leads to completion and celebration; celebration prepares us for the next cycle.

But the actual movement is rarely perfectly linear. We may spiral back — needing to pause again during attunement, or recognizing new dissonance during calibration. We may linger at one stage or move quickly through another. The spiral is a map, not a mandate.

### The spiral over time

Over time, we move through many cycles. Each complete cycle resolves some dissonance, develops some new capacity, integrates some new pattern, and prepares us for the next cycle. The relationship evolves through accumulated cycles. What seemed difficult in early cycles becomes easier. What was impossible becomes possible. We return to similar stages but at higher levels of capability.

## The catalyst spiral is a practice

These are our best practices for navigating the catalyst spiral together. This is not an exhaustive list; we encourage you to adjust these and add your own.

### We locate ourselves in the spiral

Identifying where we are in the process.

- **When we sense something is happening** in our relational field, we take a moment to assess.
- **We go through the stages together:** Are we developing awareness? Recognizing dissonance? In a catalyst moment? Needing to pause? Attuning? Calibrating? Integrating? Ready to celebrate?
- **We share what we sense:** "I think we're in a catalyst moment." "This feels like we need to pause."
- **We let location inform our response.** Knowing where we are helps us know what's needed.

*When to use: When we sense something is happening between us. When we feel lost or disoriented. When we want to respond appropriately to where we actually are.*

---

### We give each stage what it needs

Different stages call for different responses.

- **Awareness:** More sensing, less fixing. Receptive presence.
- **Dissonance:** Naming without blaming. "I notice we're out of sync."
- **Catalyst moment:** Presence to the charge. Acknowledgment of stakes.
- **Pause:** Breath. Space. Slowing down. Interrupting reactivity.
- **Attunement:** Deep listening. Seeking to understand. Realigning.
- **Calibration:** Specificity. Concrete commitments. Practical planning.
- **Integration:** Consistent practice. Patience. Mutual support.
- **Celebration:** Appreciation. Honest assessment. Intention-setting.

We offer what the stage actually needs — not what we wish were needed, not what another stage would need. This is where knowing our location matters.

*When to use: Once we've located ourselves. When we want to respond skillfully to the current stage.*

---

### We navigate transitions

Moving from one stage to the next.

- **We notice when a stage feels complete.** There's often a natural sense of being ready for what's next.
- **We name the transition:** "It feels like we've paused enough — I think we're ready to attune." "I think we've attuned enough to start calibrating."
- **We check with each other:** "Does it feel like we're ready to move on?" One of us might need more time at the current stage.
- **We allow organic movement.** We don't force transitions, but we do name them when they seem to be happening.

*When to use: When we sense a stage is completing. When we're not sure if we're ready for the next stage. When we want to move consciously through the spiral.*

---

### We work with being stuck

Sometimes we get stuck at a stage and can't seem to move through.

- **We identify where we're stuck.** Which stage can't we seem to move through?
- **We explore what's blocking us:** Stuck in dissonance — maybe we haven't fully recognized or named it. Stuck at catalyst moment — maybe we haven't really paused. Stuck in pause — maybe we're avoiding engagement. Stuck in attunement — maybe we're not really listening. Stuck in calibration — maybe we're being too vague. Stuck in integration — maybe we need more support or structure.
- **We go deeper at this stage.** What does this specific stage need that we haven't given it?
- **We consider going back.** Sometimes we're stuck because we skipped something earlier. We might need to return to a previous stage.

*When to use: When we feel stuck or blocked. When the spiral isn't moving. When the same stage keeps recurring without resolution.*

---

### We review completed cycles

When we've moved through a complete cycle, we honor and learn from it.

- **We trace the cycle we just completed:** What dissonance did we recognize? What was the catalyst moment? How did we pause, attune, calibrate, integrate?
- **We identify what worked:** What did we do well? Where did we navigate skillfully?
- **We identify what was difficult:** Where did we struggle? What stages were challenging?
- **We extract lessons:** What do we want to remember for next time?
- **We appreciate the complete cycle:** We've done something significant. We've moved through a full spiral of transformation together.

*When to use: When a cycle feels complete. When we want to learn from what we've been through. As regular reflective practice.*

---

### We build spiral awareness over time

Making the spiral a way of life rather than just a tool.

- **We practice seeing our relational life through spiral awareness** — noticing the stages as they occur.
- **We develop instinct** for where we are and what's needed, not through analysis but through developed perception.
- **We trust the spiral as a reliable process.** We've been through cycles before. We know transformation has a shape.
- **We approach the spiral with curiosity rather than dread.** Each cycle is an opportunity for growth.
- **We appreciate that the spiral never ends.** As long as we are in relationship, there will be new awareness to develop, new dissonance to recognize, new catalysts to navigate.

*When to use: As ongoing orientation. When we want the spiral to become natural rather than effortful. When we want to approach transformation as an ongoing journey rather than a series of crises.*

## Working with challenges

**When we're in different stages.** Sometimes one of us is ready to calibrate while the other hasn't finished attuning.

**What we've found helps:**

- **Name the difference:** "I think I'm at a different stage than you."
- **Go with whoever needs more time** at the earlier stage.
- **You can't pull someone forward in the spiral** — they need to complete each stage before moving on.
- **Patience here prevents having to circle back later.**

---

**When catalyst moments feel overwhelming.** The intensity can be too much.

**What we've found helps:**

- **Remember that pause is always available.** You don't have to stay in the intensity.
- **Taking space is a legitimate response.**
- **If one person is overwhelmed,** the other can hold calm presence.
- **Come back to the catalyst when you have more capacity.** The moment will wait.

---

**When we keep getting stuck at the same stage.** Some stage repeatedly blocks us.

**What we've found helps:**

- **This is important information.** The stage where we consistently get stuck reveals something about our relational pattern.
- **Explore it with curiosity:** Why does this stage trip us up? What would it take to move through it?
- **Consider whether the stage itself is where we need to focus practice,** or whether something from an earlier stage keeps undermining us.

---

**When integration keeps failing.** We calibrate but can't seem to make changes stick.

**What we've found helps:**

- **Integration is often the longest stage** and needs the most patience.
- **Make the changes smaller and more specific.**
- **Build in more check-ins and support structures.**
- **Expect setbacks and plan for how we'll handle them.**
- **Patterns that formed over years won't change in weeks.**

---

**When we're cycling too fast.** We seem to be rushing through the spiral without real transformation.

**What we've found helps:**

- **Slow down.** Linger longer at each stage.
- **Make sure each stage is actually complete** before moving on.
- **Fast cycling often means we're skipping something** — usually pause or attunement.
- **Real transformation takes time.**

---

**When we skip celebration.** We complete cycles but never pause to honor them.

**What we've found helps:**

- **Make celebration deliberate.** Schedule it.
- **What we don't celebrate, we don't encode as significant.**
- **Celebration isn't optional** — it's how we mark completion and prepare for what's next.
- **Even brief acknowledgment matters.**

## The larger view

The Catalyst Spiral is more than a tool for navigating difficult moments. It's a map of how relationship evolves — the recurring journey through which two people become more capable of being together.

Every relationship will encounter dissonance. Every relationship will face catalyst moments. The question isn't whether these will come, but how we'll meet them. The spiral gives us a way to meet them consciously — to participate in our own transformation rather than simply being changed by circumstance.

Over time, the cycles accumulate. Each one leaves us more skilled, more connected, more capable. What felt impossible in early cycles becomes possible. The relationship deepens not despite the challenges but through them.

This is the nature of living relationship: not constant harmony, but continuous growth through the rhythm of dissonance and reconnection, challenge and integration, transformation and celebration.
