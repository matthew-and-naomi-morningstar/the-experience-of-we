# Threshold 4: Navigating Challenge

*Working with friction, shadows, triggers, trauma, and different capacities.*

Every relationship meets what's hard. Differences in how we see, things we don't want to admit about ourselves, moments when something old arrives in the room with us, gaps between what one person can hold and what the other needs, inheritances of pain that neither of us caused but both of us carry. These don't go away with depth — depth makes them surface more vividly.

This threshold is for what happens then. Not avoidance, not heroic overcoming, but a steady practice of meeting friction as data, exploring what we've been disowning, recognizing when our nervous systems are time-traveling, and tending the differences in capacity that are always present between two people. The practices here protect the relationship from mistaking difficulty for incompatibility.
