# Threshold 8: Emergence

*What becomes possible when the foundation is solid.*

The early thresholds protect us from the most common harms two people can do to each other. The middle thresholds build what makes the relationship resilient under challenge and capable of repair. This threshold is for what arrives when those are in place — the kinds of aliveness that can only emerge when the conditions for them have been carefully tended.

Some of these practices look like what the world calls love — co-metabolizing reality together, building a shared story, real gratitude that returns rather than merely receives. Others look like what the world calls being alive — play, embodiment, grief, rest, becoming who we each are without growing apart. None of these arrive on demand. They emerge. The threshold is for noticing when they do and tending them well.

Two sub-thresholds, twelve practices.
