# Threshold 2: Tending the Ground

*The physical, informational, emotional, and economic ecology around the relationship.*

The conditions around a relationship shape what is possible inside it. We can practice presence, repair, and depth all we want, but if our bodies are exhausted, our minds are captured by information designed to colonize attention, and our economic ground is unstable, the relationship absorbs costs that drain what we have to give each other. Modern conditions have severed many of the bonds that once made shared life nourishing; this threshold is the practice-level response to that severance.

What we eat, how we move and sleep, how we hold our shared spaces, what we let into our minds, how we navigate money and time, what we draw from and give back to the natural world — all of these are things we do *with* and *for* each other. Not productivity work, not life optimization, but relational care. The ground we share is the medium our love lives in.
