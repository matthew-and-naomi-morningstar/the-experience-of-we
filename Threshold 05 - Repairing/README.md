# Threshold 5: Repairing

*Healing ruptures, taking responsibility, releasing what was done.*

No relationship avoids rupture. Even with good ground, even with depth, even with skill in navigating challenge, there will be moments where what we did or didn't do caused real harm to the person we are committed to. Repair is what we do then. Not apology as performance. Not making up by moving on. The slower, more honest work of acknowledging what happened, taking responsibility without collapse, calibrating action with what we now understand, and eventually releasing the residue so the relationship is not still living inside the rupture.

These practices protect the relationship from mistaking surface apology for actual repair, and from mistaking the absence of conflict for its resolution. Without them, ruptures accumulate as scar tissue. With them, ruptures often become the places where the relationship becomes more resilient than it was before.
