# The Covenant of Entry

*into The Experience of We*

---

## The Choice of Entry

We enter The Experience of We by choice.

We acknowledge that separation is a real and embodied human experience, and that Reunion is not given to us automatically. Reunion is a path we choose, and a practice we return to.

By entering this covenant, we choose to orient ourselves toward Reunion — with ourselves, with one another, and with the living systems we participate in.

This covenant marks a beginning, not an arrival.

~Matthew and Naomi Danielle Morningstar

---

## Our Foundational Agreements

By entering The Experience of We, we acknowledge and affirm the following shared commitments:

1. **Fundamental Respect**
   We agree to treat each other with fundamental respect as an ongoing relational practice, regardless of emotional state, conflict, or difference.

2. **Consent**
   We acknowledge and affirm our shared responsibility to honor consent in all its forms — spoken and unspoken, changing and contextual — as an ongoing practice.

3. **Boundaries**
   We acknowledge and affirm a shared responsibility to name, honor, and negotiate boundaries, recognizing that boundaries preserve distinction and make our relationship possible.

4. **Shared Accountability for Impact**
   We acknowledge and affirm shared accountability for the impact of our actions, words, and ways of being on one another and on the relationship itself.

5. **Good Faith Collaboration**
   We agree to collaborate with one another in good faith and with shared positive regard, even when we are confused, activated, or in disagreement.

6. **Unconscious Patterns**
   We acknowledge that unconscious patterns, wounds, and adaptations will arise in our relationship. We commit to bringing these patterns into shared awareness and working with them together, rather than denying, hiding, or weaponizing them.

7. **Conscious Uncoupling**
   We acknowledge and affirm that if the relationship no longer serves the wellbeing of one or more of us, we may consciously uncouple. We commit to honoring all of these agreements throughout the process of uncoupling, recognizing that endings are also relational acts.

---

## The Nature of this Covenant

This covenant is not a test to be passed or failed.

It is a shared point of return.

We understand and accept that sometimes we will forget, stumble, rupture, and lose our way.

When this happens, we agree that this covenant remains available to us as a place of re-orientation.

We can come back to these agreements any time we need to call ourselves or each other back onto the path.

The capacity to return is not a weakness.

It is the grace of the system.

---

## Our Ongoing Responsibility

By entering The Experience of We, we accept shared responsibility for the health, integrity, and vitality of the relationship as a living system — a relational field.

We understand that our relational field is not something we possess.

It is something we participate in, steward, and continually co-create together.

This covenant lives only through practice.

---

## The Spirit of Entry

We enter this covenant not because we are healed, certain, or complete — but because we are willing.

We are choosing this path of our own free will.

Willing to stay present.

Willing to be accountable.

Willing to choose Reunion again and again.

Welcome to The Experience of We.

## The Personal Covenant of Entry

I choose the path of Reunion.

I enter this relationship with respect, consent, and care for boundaries.

I take shared responsibility for my impact and commit to good-faith collaboration.

I acknowledge that unconscious patterns will arise, and I am willing to work with them together.

If we must part, I will do so consciously and with integrity.

When I lose my way, I will return.
