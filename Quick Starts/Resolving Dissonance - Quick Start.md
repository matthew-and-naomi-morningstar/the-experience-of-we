# Resolving Dissonance: *A Quick Start*

---

There's a particular feeling when something is off between us.

Maybe we can't quite name it. A subtle tension. A slight distance. Communication feels harder than it should. Or maybe it's louder: a friction that has become conflict, a disagreement that won't land, a collision of needs.

This is dissonance. The felt sense of being out of sync.

Dissonance is not bad. It is information. Something in the relational field needs attention. And here's the part most of us were never taught: dissonance generates energy. Real energy. Real charge. And that energy has to go somewhere.

In most relationships, the default route is defense. The energy flows into protecting our positions, building our case, withdrawing to preserve ourselves. Disagreement is what this looks like downstream — two defended positions colliding. The energy is spent, but nothing gets understood.

There is another route. The same energy can flow into resolution — into the work of understanding what the friction is trying to communicate. The energy doesn't disappear. It finds a different path. And over time, as the new route becomes practiced, dissonance stops being threatening. It becomes the way the field tells us what it needs.

---

## One practice to try

### Name it, then choose the route

When you feel something off between you, don't wait for it to become a problem.

- **Notice the feeling.** Tightness in the chest. A knot in the stomach. Something not quite right. Trust the body's signal.

- **Name it simply.** "I notice something feels off between us." "I'm sensing some tension." No blame. No certainty required. Just the naming.

- **Feel where the energy wants to go.** This is the moment. Is it flowing into defense — building your case, protecting your position, preparing your argument? Just notice. The noticing itself is the choice point.

- **Choose the other route.** Get curious together. "What is this about? What might be underneath it?" Not to fix, not to win. To understand.

- **Let the naming be enough when it is.** Sometimes curiosity and naming shift things on their own. Sometimes you'll need to go deeper. Let the moment tell you which.

One moment of friction, caught early and routed toward understanding instead of defense. That's how the energy of dissonance becomes the energy of connection.
